Að kanna Europeana

In addition to standard search options, Europeana offers alternative ways to explore and navigate through our millions of objects. You can discover Europe's rich heritage by searching:

  • With curatorial expertise: Our virtual exhibitions help you discover and learn more about specific themes, e.g. Art Nouveau or musical instruments. With extensive curatorial information that guides you through the themes, the virtual exhibitions are displayed on the image carousel of Europeana's landing page, and can be also found in the 'Kanna' tab at the top of the page.
  • Through newest content: See the latest contributions from our partner museums, libraries, archives and audio-visual archives by clicking 'Nýtt efni' in the 'Kanna' tab at the top of the page.
  • Eftir efnisveitu: Click 'By Provider' in the 'Kanna' tab at the top of the page to browse through the collections from a specific institution and open doors to any museum, library, archive and audio-visual archive that provides content to Europeana.
  • Í tímans rás: Gagnvirka Tímaás gerir þér kleyft að flokka hluti eftir dagsetningu. Þetta skiptist í þrjú svæði sem hægt er að hreyfa með því að smella og draga þau til með músinni til að fletta gegnum safn Europeana. Með efsta ásnum er auðveldlega hægt að stökkva á það tímabil sem þú hefur áhuga á. Með ásunum í miðjunni og neðst á síðunni er hægt að fara hægar yfir. Þú getur líka slegið inn efnisorð í leitarsvæðið og séð hluti sem tengjast því. Tímaásinn sýnir fyrstu 1000 hluti í hverri leit.
  • On a map: Click on the globe icon , on the search results page, to view your results on a map. The Europeana hits are shown as purple circles, with the size of the circle indicating the amount of objects on Europeana for the given search in a specific location. As you zoom in, click on a circle to find out the exact number of objects for that location. When you click on the value, you will get a list of items that you can preview and see on Europeana's full-object display page.
    Please note that at this stage, we are only exploring the geolocation possibilities. Hence, the results for only some of the search terms on Europeana can be now displayed on a map.
