Exploring Europeana

In addition to standard search options, Europeana offers alternative ways to explore and navigate through our millions of objects. You can discover Europe's rich heritage by searching:

  • With curatorial expertise: Our virtual exhibitions help you discover and learn more about specific themes, e.g. Art Nouveau or musical instruments. With extensive curatorial information that guides you through the themes, the virtual exhibitions are displayed on the image carousel of Europeana's landing page, and can be also found in the 'Explore' tab at the top of the page.
  • Through newest content: See the latest contributions from our partner museums, libraries, archives and audio-visual archives by clicking 'Ábhar gaolmhar' in the 'Explore' tab at the top of the page.
  • By provider: Click 'By Provider' in the 'Explore' tab at the top of the page to browse through the collections from a specific institution and open doors to any museum, library, archive and audio-visual archive that provides content to Europeana.
  • Brabhsáil trí na blianta: Idirghníomhach Amlíne ligtear duit féachaint ar na míreanna go croineolaíoch. Tá trí roinn i gceist agus is féidir leat iad go léir a bhogadh trí do luchóg a chliceáil agus a tharraingt chun brabhsáil a dhéanamh ar bhailiúchán Europeana. Má úsáideann tú an líne ar bharr is féidir leat dul go tapaidh go dtí na tréimhse ama a bhfuil suim agat ann. Is féidir leat scanáil níos móille a dhéanamh leis na línte i lár agus ag bun an leathanaigh. Chomh maith leis sin, is féidir leat topaic a chur sa bhosca cuardaigh chun míreanna a fheiceáil a bhaineann leis an téarma cuardaigh. I gcás cuardaigh ar bith, tugann an Amlíne duit an chéad mhíle mír.
  • On a map: Click on the globe icon , on the search results page, to view your results on a map. The Europeana hits are shown as purple circles, with the size of the circle indicating the amount of objects on Europeana for the given search in a specific location. As you zoom in, click on a circle to find out the exact number of objects for that location. When you click on the value, you will get a list of items that you can preview and see on Europeana's full-object display page.
    Please note that at this stage, we are only exploring the geolocation possibilities. Hence, the results for only some of the search terms on Europeana can be now displayed on a map.
