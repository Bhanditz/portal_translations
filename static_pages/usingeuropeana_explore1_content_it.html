Esplora Europeana

In addition to standard search options, Europeana offers alternative ways to explore and navigate through our millions of objects. You can discover Europe's rich heritage by searching:

  • With curatorial expertise: Our virtual exhibitions help you discover and learn more about specific themes, e.g. Art Nouveau or musical instruments. With extensive curatorial information that guides you through the themes, the virtual exhibitions are displayed on the image carousel of Europeana's landing page, and can be also found in the 'Esplora' tab at the top of the page.
  • Attraverso i contenuti più recenti: Guarda gli ultimi contributi dei nostri partner, musei, biblioteche, archivi e archivi audiovisivi, cliccando ' Nuovi contenuti' in the 'Esplora' tab at the top of the page.
  • Fornitori di contenuti: Click 'By Provider' in the 'Esplora' tab at the top of the page to browse through the collections from a specific institution and open doors to any museum, library, archive and audio-visual archive that provides content to Europeana.
  • cronologia: lo strumento interattivo Cronologia consente di visualizzare gli oggetti classificati per data. Far scorrere il cursore sul navigatore cronologico per navigare tra gli oggetti utilizzando la visualizzazione a carosello. Cliccare su un oggetto per visualizzare maggiori dettagli. E’ anche possibile immettere una data nella casella di ricerca.
  • On a map: Click on the globe icon , on the search results page, to view your results on a map. The Europeana hits are shown as purple circles, with the size of the circle indicating the amount of objects on Europeana for the given search in a specific location. As you zoom in, click on a circle to find out the exact number of objects for that location. When you click on the value, you will get a list of items that you can preview and see on Europeana's full-object display page.
    Please note that at this stage, we are only exploring the geolocation possibilities. Hence, the results for only some of the search terms on Europeana can be now displayed on a map.
